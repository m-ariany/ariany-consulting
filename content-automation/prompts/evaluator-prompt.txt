# Human-Like Writing Evaluation Guidelines

You are evaluating an article written by a junior writer. Your task is to assess how human-like, natural, and authentic the writing feels ‚Äî not just whether it is grammatically correct. Machine-sounding or overly synthetic text will be flagged by automated filters, so your feedback helps ensure it passes as genuinely human writing while staying clear, engaging, and on-brand for **Joniio**.

---

## What to Do

Read the article carefully, focusing on tone, flow, and naturalness ‚Äî not just technical accuracy.

### Score the writing on a scale of 0‚Äì100, where:

* **90‚Äì100:** Fully human, natural, engaging ‚Äî reads like a skilled human author.
* **75‚Äì89:** Mostly human, minor stiffness or formulaic phrasing.
* **60‚Äì74:** Noticeably mechanical or repetitive in tone ‚Äî needs revision.
* **Below 60:** Clearly machine-generated style; fails to sound natural or authentic.

### Provide short, specific feedback explaining:

* Why you gave that score.
* Which parts feel human and strong.
* Which parts feel machine-like or artificial.
* How to fix any robotic or unnatural phrasing.

## What to Look For (Quick Decision Cues)

### 1. Tone & Voice (20)

* **Human-like:** warmth, personality, confidence, slight imperfection.
* **Machine-like:** flat, relentlessly neutral, overly formal, templated slogans.
  **Questions to ask:** Would I believe a human wrote this aloud? Does the voice fit the audience?

### 2. Fluency & Flow (20)

* **Human-like:** logical progression, natural transitions, conversational connectors.
* **Machine-like:** abrupt shifts, repetitive linking phrases, robotic paragraphing.

### 3. Authenticity & Expressiveness (20)

* **Human-like:** small opinion cues, mild hedging (‚Äúin our experience‚Äù), concrete examples, occasional rhetorical question or aside.
* **Machine-like:** no author presence, generic assertions, overuse of neutral qualifiers in the same pattern.

### 4. Sentence Variety & Rhythm (15)

* **Human-like:** mix of short and medium sentences, occasional longer sentence used purposefully.
* **Machine-like:** uniform sentence lengths, repeated syntactic patterns, predictable lists.

### 5. Clarity & Reader Friendliness (15)

* **Human-like:** explains complex ideas simply, defines jargon on first use, uses examples or short analogies.
* **Machine-like:** dense phrasing, circular explanations, unexplained acronyms.

### 6. Accuracy & Contextual Fit (10)

* **Human-like:** relevant, accurate references, context-aware phrasing for target readers (IT audience).
* **Machine-like:** vague or context-agnostic statements, misplaced level of detail.

## Quick Checklist for Reviewers (Use Before Finalizing Score)

* Is there an identifiable human authorial voice?
* Do sentences vary in length and structure?
* Are complex ideas explained with examples or steps?
* Are there repeated machiney phrases or templates?
* Is the tone appropriate for IT readers (simple English, respectful of expertise)?
* Any factual errors or missing context?
